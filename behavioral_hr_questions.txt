BEHAVIORAL AND HR INTERVIEW QUESTIONS — KNOWLEDGE BASE
=======================================================

STAR METHOD FRAMEWORK
---------------------
STAR stands for: Situation, Task, Action, Result.
Use this framework to structure behavioral answers:
- Situation: Describe the context or background
- Task: Explain your responsibility or role
- Action: Detail the specific steps YOU took
- Result: Quantify the outcome or impact

COMMON BEHAVIORAL QUESTIONS AND FRAMEWORKS

Q: Tell me about yourself.
Framework: Present-Past-Future formula.
- Present: Current role and key responsibilities
- Past: Relevant experience and key achievements
- Future: Why this role excites you and your goals
Tip: Keep it under 2 minutes. Tailor to the job description.

Q: What are your greatest strengths?
Framework: Identify 2-3 strengths relevant to the role. Back each with a concrete example.
Tip: Align strengths with the job requirements. Avoid generic answers like "hardworking."

Q: What is your greatest weakness?
Framework: Name a real (non-critical) weakness, explain steps you are taking to improve, show progress.
Tip: Never say "I work too hard." Pick something genuine and demonstrate self-awareness.

Q: Describe a time you faced a major challenge at work.
STAR Example:
- Situation: Project was 3 weeks behind schedule due to team member illness.
- Task: I was the lead developer responsible for delivery.
- Action: Reorganized sprint backlog, brought in contractor, worked overtime strategically.
- Result: Delivered on time. Client extended contract by 6 months.

Q: Tell me about a time you worked in a team and faced conflict.
Framework: Focus on resolution, not blame. Show empathy, communication skills, and outcome.
Tip: Never speak negatively about teammates. Show how conflict improved the outcome.

Q: Give an example of a time you showed leadership.
Framework: You don't need a management title. Leadership can be influencing, mentoring, or driving initiative.
Tip: Quantify the impact of your leadership.

Q: Tell me about a time you failed.
Framework: Own the failure, explain what you learned, show what you changed.
Tip: Choose a genuine example. Interviewers respect honesty and growth mindset.

Q: Where do you see yourself in 5 years?
Framework: Align ambition with realistic growth in the company. Show loyalty + ambition.
Tip: Research the company's growth paths before the interview.

Q: Why do you want to work here?
Framework: Research the company. Mention specific projects, culture, mission, or products.
Tip: Never say "for the salary" or "because it's a good company." Be specific.

Q: Why should we hire you?
Framework: Match your top 3 skills to the top 3 job requirements. Add unique value.
Tip: Summarize your pitch: "I bring X, Y, Z which directly maps to your needs A, B, C."

Q: How do you handle stress and pressure?
Framework: Give a real example. Mention techniques: prioritization, time blocking, communication.
Tip: Show you stay calm and productive. Never say "I don't get stressed."

Q: Describe your ideal work environment.
Framework: Research company culture. Balance honesty with compatibility.
Tip: Show flexibility. Mention collaboration, feedback, and learning.

HR POLICY QUESTIONS

Q: What are your salary expectations?
Framework: Do market research. Give a range, not a fixed number. Express flexibility.
Tip: Say "Based on my research and experience, I'm looking at $X to $Y, but I'm open to discussion."

Q: Are you willing to relocate or travel?
Framework: Be honest. If yes, express enthusiasm. If partial, give conditions.

Q: When can you start?
Framework: Give standard notice period. Offer to negotiate if needed.

Q: Do you have any questions for us?
ALWAYS ask questions. Suggested questions:
- What does success look like in the first 90 days?
- How would you describe the team culture?
- What are the biggest challenges someone in this role faces?
- What are the growth opportunities?
- How is performance reviewed?

FRESHER-SPECIFIC QUESTIONS

Q: Why did you choose this field/major?
Framework: Passion, curiosity, early experiences, or influential people.

Q: Do you have any internship or project experience?
Framework: Describe the project, your role, technologies used, and what you learned.

Q: How have your studies prepared you for this role?
Framework: Map coursework and projects directly to job responsibilities.

Q: What extracurricular activities have you participated in?
Framework: Leadership roles, competitions, clubs. Show soft skills and initiative.

EXPERIENCED PROFESSIONAL QUESTIONS

Q: Walk me through your career progression.
Framework: Chronological but highlight jumps in responsibility and impact.
Tip: Show a logical story of growth.

Q: Why are you leaving your current job?
Framework: Keep it positive. Focus on growth opportunities, not problems.
Tip: Never badmouth current employer.

Q: What is your management style?
Framework: Situational leadership. Adapt to individual needs. Give examples.

Q: How do you keep your skills up to date?
Framework: Mention courses, certifications, communities, side projects, reading.
