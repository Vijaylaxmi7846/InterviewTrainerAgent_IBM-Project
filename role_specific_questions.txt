ROLE-SPECIFIC INTERVIEW QUESTIONS
===================================

PRODUCT MANAGER ROLE

Q: How do you prioritize features in a product roadmap?
Frameworks: RICE (Reach, Impact, Confidence, Effort), MoSCoW (Must/Should/Could/Won't), Kano Model, Impact vs Effort matrix.
Model Answer: "I use a combination of RICE scoring for quantitative prioritization and stakeholder interviews to understand strategic alignment. I ensure we balance quick wins with long-term investments."

Q: How do you define success for a product feature?
Answer: Define clear KPIs (activation rate, retention, NPS, revenue impact) before launch. Use OKRs to tie features to company objectives.

Q: How do you work with engineering teams?
Answer: I create detailed PRDs (Product Requirement Documents), hold regular sprint planning, daily standups, and ensure clear acceptance criteria. I act as the bridge between business and tech.

Q: Walk me through a product you improved.
STAR Framework: Identify the problem with data, hypothesize solution, design MVP, test with users, measure results, iterate.

Q: How do you handle stakeholder disagreements?
Answer: Listen to understand, use data to support decisions, find common ground, escalate only when necessary. Focus on shared goals.

DATA ANALYST ROLE

Q: How would you approach a drop in a key metric?
Framework:
1. Verify the data (is the drop real or a tracking issue?)
2. Segment by time, geography, device, user type
3. Check for external factors (seasonality, outages, campaigns)
4. Form hypotheses, test them
5. Root cause analysis, propose solution

Q: Explain the difference between mean, median, and mode.
Mean: Average (sensitive to outliers). Median: Middle value (robust to outliers). Mode: Most frequent value. Use median for skewed distributions.

Q: What SQL query would you write to find the top 5 customers by revenue?
Answer: SELECT customer_id, SUM(revenue) as total_revenue FROM orders GROUP BY customer_id ORDER BY total_revenue DESC LIMIT 5;

Q: How do you handle missing data?
Strategies: Drop rows (if small %), impute with mean/median/mode, use model-based imputation (KNN, regression), flag with indicator variable.

Q: What is A/B testing and how do you design one?
Answer: Controlled experiment comparing two versions. Steps: Define hypothesis, choose metric, calculate sample size (power analysis), randomize assignment, run for statistical significance, analyze with t-test/chi-square, make decision.

MARKETING ROLE

Q: How do you measure the success of a marketing campaign?
Metrics: Impressions, CTR, CPL, CPA, CAC, ROAS, LTV:CAC ratio, NPS, brand sentiment.
Framework: Set goals → choose metrics → track → analyze → optimize.

Q: What is your experience with digital marketing channels?
Channels: SEO, SEM/PPC, social media (organic/paid), email, content marketing, influencer, affiliate, programmatic display.

Q: How do you create a buyer persona?
Answer: Combine qualitative (interviews, surveys) and quantitative (analytics, CRM data) research. Include demographics, psychographics, pain points, goals, preferred channels, buying triggers.

Q: Describe a successful campaign you ran.
STAR: Set objective → designed creative → chose channels → executed → measured results with specific numbers (ROI, conversions, reach).

FINANCE ROLE

Q: Walk me through a DCF analysis.
Answer:
1. Project free cash flows (FCF) for 5-10 years
2. Determine discount rate (WACC for firms, required return for equity)
3. Calculate terminal value (Gordon Growth Model or Exit Multiple)
4. Discount all cash flows to present value
5. Sum to get enterprise value; subtract debt, add cash for equity value

Q: What is the difference between EBITDA and net income?
EBITDA: Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, Amortization — proxy for operating cash flow, ignores capital structure.
Net Income: Bottom line after all expenses. EBITDA is used for valuation comparisons across companies.

Q: Explain the three financial statements and how they link.
Income Statement: Revenue, expenses, net income (period performance).
Balance Sheet: Assets, liabilities, equity (snapshot in time). Net income flows to retained earnings.
Cash Flow Statement: Operating, investing, financing activities. Net income reconciled to cash via indirect method.

HUMAN RESOURCES ROLE

Q: How do you handle an underperforming employee?
Framework: PIPs (Performance Improvement Plans), regular 1:1s, clear KPIs, coaching, training. Document everything. Follow HR policy.

Q: What strategies do you use for talent acquisition?
Answer: Job analysis → sourcing (LinkedIn, job boards, referrals, campus) → screening → structured interviews → assessment centers → offer → onboarding.

Q: How do you promote diversity and inclusion?
Answer: Blind resume screening, diverse interview panels, inclusive job descriptions, ERGs (Employee Resource Groups), pay equity audits, unconscious bias training.

SALES ROLE

Q: How do you handle a customer objection?
Framework: Listen → Acknowledge → Clarify → Respond → Confirm. Never argue. Turn objections into opportunities.

Q: Walk me through your sales process.
Standard: Prospecting → Qualification (BANT: Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline) → Discovery → Presentation → Objection handling → Closing → Follow-up.

Q: How do you build a pipeline from scratch?
Answer: ICP (Ideal Customer Profile) definition, multi-channel outreach (cold email, LinkedIn, phone), partner referrals, inbound marketing, events.

FRESHER SPECIFIC TECHNICAL QUESTIONS

Q: What projects have you worked on?
Framework: Describe the problem, your tech stack, your specific role, challenges faced, and outcomes. Deploy to GitHub and have demos ready.

Q: What programming languages do you know?
Tip: Be honest. Rank by proficiency. Mention specific projects for each.

Q: How do you approach learning a new technology?
Framework: Mention structured learning (docs, courses), hands-on projects, community engagement, pair programming.

SENIOR PROFESSIONAL SPECIFIC QUESTIONS

Q: How do you mentor junior team members?
Framework: Regular 1:1s, code reviews, pair programming, growth plans, psychological safety, recognizing achievements.

Q: Describe your experience leading cross-functional teams.
Answer: Stakeholder alignment, clear RACI, communication cadence, managing conflicts, celebrating wins.

Q: How do you drive innovation in your team?
Framework: Psychological safety, hackathons, 20% time, retrospectives, learning from failures.
