INTERVIEW TIPS AND GUIDELINES
==============================

BEFORE THE INTERVIEW

Research Checklist:
- Company mission, vision, values
- Recent news, product launches, acquisitions
- Competitors and market position
- Team/department you are joining
- Interviewer's LinkedIn profile (if known)
- Glassdoor reviews and interview experiences
- Job description: Highlight required vs preferred skills

Preparation:
- Practice your "Tell me about yourself" (under 2 minutes)
- Prepare 5-7 STAR stories covering: leadership, conflict, failure, success, teamwork, pressure
- Research salary range (Glassdoor, LinkedIn Salary, Levels.fyi for tech)
- Prepare 5 thoughtful questions for interviewers
- Mock interviews with friends or tools

Logistics:
- Confirm time zone and interview format (video/in-person)
- Test camera, microphone, and internet (for virtual)
- Dress appropriately — when in doubt, overdress
- Arrive 10 minutes early (in-person) or 5 minutes early (virtual)
- Bring multiple copies of resume (in-person)
- Have a notebook and pen

DURING THE INTERVIEW

Do:
- Make strong eye contact and smile genuinely
- Listen carefully before answering — it's OK to pause and think
- Use the STAR method for behavioral questions
- Quantify achievements wherever possible (increased by X%, saved $Y)
- Show enthusiasm for the role and company
- Ask clarifying questions if unsure
- Take notes
- Match the interviewer's energy and communication style

Don't:
- Interrupt the interviewer
- Bad-mouth previous employers
- Lie or exaggerate
- Give one-word answers
- Ramble without structure — use frameworks
- Check your phone
- Appear desperate

Communication Tips:
- Speak clearly and at moderate pace
- Use professional vocabulary
- Bridge weak points: "While I haven't done X directly, I have experience with Y which is very similar because..."
- Show coachability: "That's a great point, I would also consider..."

AFTER THE INTERVIEW

- Send a personalized thank-you email within 24 hours
- Reference specific topics discussed in the interview
- Reiterate your interest and fit
- Follow up if you don't hear back within the timeline given

If you get rejected:
- Ask for feedback (many companies will share)
- Review and improve weak areas
- Keep a pipeline of 5-10 active applications

VIRTUAL INTERVIEW SPECIFIC TIPS

Technical Setup:
- Professional background (plain wall or virtual background)
- Good lighting — light source should be in front of you
- Camera at eye level
- Headphones to avoid echo
- Have a backup (phone hotspot, phone number to call)

Virtual Body Language:
- Look at the camera, not the screen
- Sit up straight, lean slightly forward
- Nod to show engagement
- Minimize distractions — phone on silent, "Do Not Disturb" on

SALARY NEGOTIATION TIPS

- Never be the first to give a number if possible
- Use the range strategy: give a range where your target is the lower end
- Back up your number with market data
- Consider total compensation: base + bonus + equity + benefits + PTO
- It's OK to ask for time to consider an offer (24-48 hours)
- Get the offer in writing

COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

1. Not preparing specific examples (generic answers)
2. Talking too long or too short
3. Not researching the company
4. Focusing only on technical skills, ignoring soft skills
5. Not asking any questions
6. Appearing nervous without managing it (practice helps)
7. Forgetting to follow up

INTERVIEW SCORING RUBRIC (What interviewers assess)

Communication: Clarity, structure, conciseness, active listening
Technical Competence: Depth, accuracy, problem-solving approach
Cultural Fit: Alignment with values, enthusiasm, team player
Leadership/Initiative: Examples of going beyond the job description
Growth Mindset: Curiosity, willingness to learn, handling feedback

SCORING GUIDE (1-5 scale used by many companies):
5 = Exceptional — would hire immediately
4 = Strong — clear yes
3 = Adequate — meets bar
2 = Weak — below bar
1 = Poor — do not hire
